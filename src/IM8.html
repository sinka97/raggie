    Hosting and Environment Management
       
    1.1
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall host on-premise CII and SII in Government Compliant Hosting Environments (GCHEs); on-premise Internet-facing systems with any of the following properties shall also be hosted in GCHEs:
       
      a) Provision of financial transaction services such as payment of fees, taxes or fines;
      b) Storage of information classified as CONFIDENTIAL or higher;
      c) Display of sensitive entity information that is SENSITIVE NORMAL or higher; or
      d) Connectivity to the Government Enterprise Network (GEN). 
         
    1.1/S1**
    Level 0
    		Agencies’ hosting environments shall only be considered GCHEs when they meet the following requirements:
       
      a) Located within Singapore;
      b) Possess redundant telecom exchange links to prevent single points of failure that can bring down entire networks;
      c) Separate Government resources from non-Government resources through means such as:
         
        i)     Host Government resources in dedicated physical and network segments;
        ii) Implement separate networks and WAN links connecting to Internet Service Provider (ISP) point of presence (POP);
        iii) House hardware appliances, such as servers and network appliances, in separate racks;
        iv) Host Government websites in separate web servers; and
        v) Use separate virtualisation infrastructure and hypervisors.
           
      d) Audited through GovTech audit programmes;
       
      e) Implement personnel security controls that include:
         
        i) Checks to ensure prohibited items are not brought into the hosting environment; and
        ii) Entry for only personnel who are both Internal Security Department (ISD) security-cleared and authorised by the Government into the hosting environment;
         
      f) Implement physical security controls that include:
         
        i) Continuous monitoring (24 hours a day and 7 days a week) of physical environmental logs, such as door access, CCTV footage, and ambient temperature; and
        ii) Triggers to alert designated personnel so that they can immediately respond to security incidents.
           
      g) Implement IT security controls that include:
         
        i) Monitor of network traffic by WOG ICT Services;
        ii) Denial-of-Service (DoS) mitigation measures;
        iii) Vulnerability assessment (VA) scans; and
        iv)     Log and monitor security-related events to facilitate detection and investigation of security incidents.
           
    1.1/S2**
    Level 0
    		For Agencies hosting their systems in facilities that are identified as CIs/ High Profile Developments (HPD), Agencies shall adhere to FCM M15/2018.
         
    1.2
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall manage and configure their networks, endpoints, and software securely to protect their systems against cybersecurity threats.
       
    1.2/S1C11
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall define and apply secure configuration profiles to secure their systems. For CII and SII, Agencies shall review their configuration annually. In addition, in respect of the following types of CII assets as they may be found in the CII, the CII owners shall establish and implement a security configuration baseline for each asset that is commensurate with the cybersecurity risk profile of the CII:
       
      a) Operating systems;
      b) Appliances; 
      c) Consoles and workstations, including Human Machine Interfaces (HMI) and OT engineering workstations;
      d) Servers, including alarm servers, OT historians and management servers; and
      e) Any other CII asset or type of asset identified by the Commissioner.
       
    1.2/S2##C1
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall also apply the security measures below, in conjunction with secure configuration profiles to further secure their operating systems and virtualised environment:
       
      a) Disable login functionality to system-level privileged accounts, such as “root” account, where possible;
      b) Restrict switching to system level privileged accounts using software like “su”;
      c) Enable only services that are required;
      d) Remove unused or obsolete files, including backup files and virtual system images;
      e) Restrict transfer of data between hypervisors and their Guest Operating Systems; and
      f) Use separate system accounts for hypervisor and Guest Operating Systems.
       
    1.2/S3
    Level 1
    		For secure configurations that cannot be implemented, Agencies shall assess the risk, implement the necessary risk mitigation measures and seek the appropriate approving authority’s acceptance of residual risks.
       
    1.2/S4##
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall install anti-malware software in their Operating Systems, except for mainframes, to detect and remove malware; apply updates pertaining to the anti-malware software’s ability to detect and remove malware.
         
    1.2/S5##
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall synchronise the internal clocks of their systems and devices to a common reference time source, such as Network Time Protocol (NTP) service, to facilitate incident investigations.
         
    1.2/S6
    Level 0
    		For CII and SII, and/or SECRET systems that are hosted in a multi-tenant environment, Agencies shall ensure that privileged users who manage hypervisors are different from those who manage the Guest Operating Systems.
         
    1.2/S7C11
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall verify the integrity of software installation files prior to its installation. For CIIs, Agencies shall ensure that only applications that are necessary for the operation and cybersecurity of the CII and that have been approved by the CII owners are used in the CII. The CII owners shall also establish and maintain a list of such approved applications.
       
    1.2/G1
    Level 2
    		Agencies should consider restricting the use of the "su" command by configuring the "sudoers" file.
       
    1.2/G2
    This clause has been superseded by 1.2/S7.
       
    1.2/G3
    Level 2
    		Agencies should consider using physically separated hardware for systems belonging to different classifications and network zones.
       
    1.2/G4
    Level 2
    		Agencies should apply the appropriate configuration settings from the following sources for their secure configuration profiles:
       
      a) Industry consensus guidelines, such as those from the Center for Internet Security’s (CIS) Benchmarks, the National Institute of Science and Technology’s (NIST) National Checklist Program, or their equivalents; and  
      b) The latest vendor security guidance.
       
    1.3
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall secure their web browsers to protect users from web-based threats.
       
    1.3/S1
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall secure their web browsers by configuring their browsers with the following settings:
         
      a) Route internet traffic through Agencies’ approved web proxy server;
      b) Disallow users from storing password;
      c) Disallow users from installing plug-ins;
      d) Disable the use of unsigned ActiveX controls;
      e) Limit browsers’ caching and temporary files’ storage size; and
      f) Clear the cache and temporary files upon exit or regularly.
       
    1.4
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall secure resources and data that are related to their systems’ production environments.
       
    1.4/S1**
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall not use Government production resources or data in computing environments that are not managed by the Government.
       
    1.4/S2C1
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall apply the following security measures to secure production data before their use in non-production environments; Agencies shall also perform a risk assessment for the use of production data in non-production environments, and seek the appropriate approving authority’s approval of residual risks on whether the production data has been sufficiently secured for use in non-production environments:
       
      a) Ensure that the amount of data requested is not excessive;
      b) Develop processes to ensure that data is only used for their approved purposes;
      c) ## Encrypt production data prior to its transfer from production to non-production environments;
      d) ** Use authorised storage media for data transfer;
      e) Develop processes to ensure that each data transfer is authorised;
      f) Log and review the use of production data; and
      g) Securely erase production data when their use is no longer required.
         
    1.4/S3C11
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall apply the following security measures to secure their development, testing, and staging environments that are outside Government ownership:
         
      a) Desensitise data before it is used outside the Government;
      b) Implement vulnerability and patch management processes; ensure management oversight over the effectiveness of the security patch management processes;
      c) Use anti-malware software with up-to-date signatures;
      d) Secure access to these environments through controls, such as authentication and IP address whitelisting;
      e) Erase data securely, including those that have been desensitised, when their use is no longer required; and
      f) Decommission the systems used in these environments upon the end of the contracts or projects.
       
    1.4/S4##
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall segregate their production networks from their non-production networks.
       
    1.4/G1
    Level 0
    		Agencies should consider using the following techniques to desensitise data:
       
      a) Hashing with salt;
      b) Tokenisation;
      c) Field-level encryption; and
      d) Obfuscation or masking of data.
       
    1.5##
    Level 0
    		For systems that need to recover from disruptions, Agencies shall establish, implement, and test their backup processes and recovery plans to ensure that systems can be restored when required.
       
    1.5/S1C1
    Level 0
    		For systems with a defined RTO and RPO, Agencies shall implement a backup and recovery  plan which considers scenarios such as ransomware attacks and hardware failures, so that services can be recovered in the event of a system disruption or when data is corrupted or deleted. The backup plan, which is approved by IDSC, shall minimally describe measures to ensure that:
       
      a) Backups are performed at a frequency that can meet the system’s RPO;
      b) Storage locations of backups are specified;
      c) Backups are protected from unauthorised access; and
      d) Recovery tests are performed on a regular basis.
         
    1.5/S2C1
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall apply the following measures to protect their backup media:
      a) Use backup media in accordance with manufacturers’ instructions;
      b) Encrypt backup media that contain classified information or sensitive entity data;
      c)  Prevent unintended or unauthorised overwriting of the backup media;
    d) ** Store backup media in physically secured locations, different from the primary data storage; and
      e) ** Track movement of backup media.
    Agencies shall perform risk assessment and seek the appropriate approving authority’s acceptance of residual risks if Agencies are not able to meet the requirements stipulated in c), and d)’s second requirement.
         
    1.5/S3##
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall use their backup management tools to periodically back up their systems and configuration files, including firewall rules; Agencies shall also initiate backups whenever there are major changes to their systems.
         
    1.5/S4
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall test the recovery plan to ensure that the system and data can be recovered to meet its RTO and RPO.
         
    1.5/G1
    Level 2
    		Agencies should include the following considerations in their backup and recovery plans:
         
      a) Whether backups are performed using either the differential, incremental or full-backup mode;
      b) Where backups should be stored, such as location of data centres, and the server racks housing the backups; and
      c) Whether the backups are protected using encryption, or passwords.
         
    1.5/G2
    Level 2
    		Agencies should implement measures that can help prevent the unintended or unauthorised overwriting of backup media using measures. These measures can include:
         
      a) Restricting access to backups by using offline backups not accessible from the network, or implementing access control lists to manage access to backups; or
      b) Using immutable backups such as backups which use Write Once, Read Many (WORM) technologies.
         
         
    Change Management
       
    2.1
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall establish and implement change management processes to manage how changes are deployed to their systems.
       
    2.1/S1
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall define the roles and responsibilities for staff involved in their change management processes; all staff assuming these roles in the change management process, except for the Change Requester, will require the CIOs' approval.
       
    2.1/S2C11
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall establish and implement the following measures as part of their change management process:
       
      a) Document the change request details, assessments, and approvals of the change requests;
      b) Test the changes in a similar environment to that of production, before deploying the changes to production environments; and 
      c) Verify that the approved changes are deployed correctly to the production environments.
      d) Ensure rolling back of the deployed changes, including security patches are possible when required. For example, in instances where security patches led to operation issue.
         
    2.1/G1
    Level 2
    		Agencies should consider defining the following roles and responsibilities for staff involved in the Agencies’ change management processes:
       
      a) Change Requester: An individual who requests for a change to be implemented in the system;
      b) Project Manager: An individual who oversees a project and supports the change requests;
      c) Change Approver: An individual or a group of individuals who assesses and decides whether to implement change requests; and
      d) Change Builder: An individual who plans and implements the change.
       
    2.1/G2
    Level 2
    		Agencies should consider including the following details in their documentation for change requests:
       

         		 a) Purpose of change;
      b) Scheduled date, time, and duration for change; and
      c) Test plans for verifying the changes.
         
    2.1/G3
    Level 2
    		Agencies should conduct a risk assessment to determine the environment to test changes in. The environment should enable Agencies to test changes in a comprehensive manner without affecting operations. Suggested environments, listed in descending order of recommendation, are as follows:
         
    a) An environment that hosts similarly configured non-production systems. These are environments which host systems whose configurations and software versions mirror that used in Agencies’ production environments;
      b) A User Acceptance Test environment; or
      c) A segment of the production environment.
       
    IT Asset Management
       
    3.1
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall maintain their inventory lists of IT assets and keep them up-to-date so that the IT assets can be securely managed.
         
    3.1/S1**C2
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall manage their IT assets with data storage capabilities by implementing the following measures:
         
      a) Maintain inventory lists with asset information recorded;
      b) Review and update their inventory lists whenever an asset is transferred or identified for disposal; these lists have to be updated within a month after the review; and
      c) Physically check for the presence of the assets at least once every 12 months and account for those assets that are missing.
       
    3.1/S2C1
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall record the following asset information in their inventory lists:
       
      a) Name of asset owner;
      b)** Unique identification number of the asset and their data storages, including removable storage, and attached storage that are found inside the asset’s hardware;
      c) Current software and/or firmware version installed on their systems and devices; and
      d) Status of the asset’s use, such as “In use”, “To be disposed”, or “Disposed”.
       
    3.1/G1
    Level 2
    		Agencies should consider using the “IT assets inventory template” (Refer to Attachment) for developing their own IT assets inventory list.
       
    3.1/G2
    Level 2
    		Agencies should consider using the “Guide for End User Computing Devices (EUCD) Asset Management Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)” (Refer to Attachment) for developing their own “End User Computing Devices Asset Management SOP”.
       
    3.2
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall protect their IT assets that contain data from unauthorised access.
     
    3.2/S1**C2
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall implement the following measures when handling the disposal of IT assets with data storage capabilities, such as networking devices, network appliances, and storage media that contain data:
       
      a) Store the assets in a physically secured area that is only accessible by authorised personnel;
      b) Monitor and record the activities in the secured area using Closed Circuit Television (CCTV), and ensure that the recordings can be used to identify the people who enter into the area and observe all their activities;
      c) Verify the identity of authorised personnel and visitors hosted by authorised personnel, and record information about their access, which includes their name, organisation, reason for access, time and date of access, and duration of stay;
      d) Escort all visitors within the secured area;
      e) Review on a monthly basis the list of personnel who are authorised to enter the secured area to identify personnel whose access rights are no longer required; these access rights shall be removed within five working days from the completion of the review; and
      f) Conduct a stocktake on a quarterly basis on the assets that are identified for disposal so that stolen or lost assets can be discovered. 
         
    3.2/S2**C1
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall ensure that the repair of their assets which contain non-removable data storage is performed under the supervision of Agencies’ authorised staff.
       
      (Not in use)
       
    3.3
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall ensure that the IT assets with data storage capabilities that have been identified for disposal or return are securely sanitised; these IT assets include those that have been loaned or leased.
       
    3.3/S1**
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall securely sanitise all hardware that stores data at rest, including those that are attached within their IT assets, using the following measures:
       
      a) Securely delete the data using industry acceptable standards:
         
        i) Peter Gutmann Secure Deletion;
        ii) Bruce Schneier Algorithm; 
        iii) US Department of Defence’s Standards (DoD 5220.22-M); or
        iv) NIST 800-88 Rev 1.
      b) Witness the sanitisation process; and
      c) Shred, disintegrate, pulverise or melt data storage that Agencies do not intend to reuse, within three months of the date from which the data storage was identified for disposal; the disposal process shall follow an established and secure chain of custody.
       
    Network Security Management
       
    4.1
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall manage and implement their network operation processes to ensure that their networks operate securely.
       
    4.1/S1##C1
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall define the roles and responsibilities of individuals managing and operating the network.
       
    4.1/S2##C1
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall establish SOPs for operating their network devices to manage requests, such as lifting firewall rules and changing routing configurations.
       
    4.1/S3**
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall house their network devices in physically secured environments, such as within data centres or within Agencies’ secured premises.
       
    4.1/S4##C2
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall document and maintain information about their network topologies and architectures, and keep them up-to-date.
       
    4.1/G1
    Level 2
    		Agencies should consider including the following information in the documentation about their networks:
         
      a) Network topology diagrams that describe the arrangement of elements in a network;
      b) Network architecture diagrams that describe how data flows in a network;
      c) Information about each VLAN segment, such as Server VLAN, Contractor VLAN, Agency User VLAN, and the purposes of each VLAN segment; and
      d) Host information, such as IP addresses and physical host location.
         
    4.2
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall architect and implement their networks to prevent unauthorised network access.
       
    4.2/S1##
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall architect their networks by doing the following:
         
      a) Zone and segregate networks;
      b) Deny network traffic by default;
      c) Inspect all network traffic travelling into, within, or out of Agencies’ networks; and
      d) Use dedicated network-based and/or application firewalls for their perimeters; Agencies shall ensure that these firewalls do not provide other unrelated services, such as business applications, email services, VPN services, and web proxy functions.
       
    4.2/S2**C2
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall use physical application firewalls to separate:
         
      a) (Not in use)
      b) Internet-facing segments from intranet segments.
       
    4.2/S3
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall implement the following network security controls to detect and protect their network resources from unauthorised access:
           
      a) Network-based firewalls to block both unauthorised inbound and outbound network traffic;
      b) Host-based firewalls to protect endpoint devices against unauthorised network connections, where possible;
      c) Application firewalls to filter unauthorised application and service requests, whenever required;
      d) Intrusion Detection/Prevention Systems (IDS/IPS) to identify malicious traffic to/from public networks, such as the Internet or Business Partner-Managed Networks; and
      e) Web proxies to monitor and control the use of Internet.
         
    4.2/S4C11
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall ensure that their NIDS/NIPS are placed in front of the nearest network perimeter firewall and implement network security mechanisms between the different network segments to detect and block malicious network traffic and to secure the network communications.
         
    4.3
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall manage and configure their network devices to secure their networks.
       
    4.3/S1##
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall configure routers by using the following measures:
         
      a) Use access control lists (ACLs); and
      b) Disable unnecessary services or protocols.
         
    4.3/S2##
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall manage their firewalls by using the following processes:
         
      a) Engage two firewall administrators (one primary and one secondary) to administer their firewalls;
      b) Test new firewalls before their deployment to the production environment;
      c) C2 Set up automated notifications to firewall administrators or Agencies’ designated personnel upon the detection of:
         
        i) Creation, modification, deletion, or disabling of firewall rules;
        ii) Unusual or sudden spike/drop in the utilisation of the firewall’s system resources;
         
      d) Perform stateful inspection on all incoming and outgoing traffic.
         
    4.3/S3##
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall group and organise their firewall rules in accordance with the following requirements to facilitate the management of their firewall rules:
         
      a) Establish and implement naming conventions for their firewall rules;
      b) Group related firewall rules; and
      c) Indicate the purpose of all firewall rules.
         
    4.3/S4##
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall create and implement their network-based firewall rules to meet the following requirements:
         
      a) Create and implement a rule (“Stealth rule”) that only allows authorised management IP addresses to communicate with the firewall, and drop all other traffic; this rule must also be at or near the top of the Agency’s rule base;
      b) Define the protocols, source and destination IP addresses, port numbers, and interfaces to be allowed for each firewall rule;
      c) Review firewall rules every 12 months, and immediately remove those that are overly permissive, obsolete, or redundant; and
      d) Create and implement a rule (“Clean-up rule”) that denies all traffic that was not allowed by the preceding rules.
         
    4.3/S5##
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall not create or implement their firewall rules that allow for the following scenarios:
         
      a) Unrestricted communication between any hosts or networks using any protocols;
      b) Outbound network traffic from an internal host, except for authorised hosts such as web proxies, that can access any external host;
      c) Inbound network traffic from any external host, except for authorised hosts such as Internet-facing services that are not further protected by reverse-proxy servers, that can access an internal host; and
      d) Any protocol usage between one or more hosts or networks, such as a lack of rules defining the types of protocols used.
       
    4.3/S6##
    Level 0
    		For external-facing network-based firewalls, Agencies shall configure their firewalls with the following settings:
       
      a) Deny incoming network packets, such as ICMP messages, that are typically used for Denial-of-Service attacks;
      b) Deny incoming packets with “private”, or “loopback” source/destination IP addresses, or “TCP NULL” packets; and
      c) Deny incoming and outgoing traffic where the source address is blacklisted or invalid.
         
    4.3/S7
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall configure their network-based firewalls to enable Network Address Translation (NAT) so that hosts from internal networks are not exposed to external networks such as the Internet or Business Partner Networks.
       
    4.3/S8##
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall manage and implement Network Intrusion Detection or Network Intrusion Prevention Systems (NIDS/NIPS) with the following measures:
         
      a) Network interfaces used for management purposes are not connected directly to the Internet and ensure that none of the management traffic traverses the Internet;
      b) NIDS/NIPS signatures are kept up-to-date;
      c) Alerts are triggered when suspicious activities are detected; and
      d) Notifications are sent to Agencies for their situational awareness.
         
    4.3/G1
    Level 2
    		Agencies should consider using the following naming convention such as the following for their firewall rules:
     
    <environment>_<project name>_<production/staging/testing>_<network zone>_<basic context>.

      f) Ensuring that the wired or wireless networks meant for access by the general public/guest is put in place as a separate network, with no physical or logical connection to the Agency Networks; and
      g) Ensuring that there are no logical or physical connections from Agency Networks to external networks (other than to the ISP for the purpose of hosting Internet-accessible Application Systems) when the Agency Networks are connected to SGNet.     5.1/S1
    Level 0
    		To allow only Agency-approved access to GEN (wired and wireless networks), Agency shall use 802.1x with both machine and user certificates authentication, except for any one of the following situations:

    5.1/S3
    Level 0
    		To minimize risks from external networks, Agencies shall subscribe to the following for accessing external networks/services from GEN:
         
      a) Business-Partner Managed Network (BMNet) connection service on behalf of their business partners, for business partner's services that need to connect to SGNet and are dedicated for Government use; 
      b) Central Extranet Services for Agency Networks that are connected to SGNet and require connectivity to external systems; or
      c) Government-approved gateways for accessing cloud services such as Infrastructure-as a Service (IaaS), Platform as a Service (PaaS) and Software as a Service (SaaS) offered by Government-approved Internet cloud provider when connecting from GEN. Examples of the Government-approved gateways are the gateways setup in Government Commercial Cloud Services (GCCS) and Government-approved secure Internet gateway.
         
    5.2
    Level 0
    		To have full oversight and control of their Agency Networks, Agencies:
       
      a) Shall not set up their Agency Networks in premises where they do not have control (such as business partners' premises); and
      b) Shall implement and manage physical security measures, including physical access control, to prevent unauthorised access to the premises where Agency Networks reside.
         
    5.3
    Level 0
    		To reduce risks of data exfiltration and malicious activities, when implementing wireless solution in Agency Networks and Business Partner Networks, Agencies shall:
         
      a) Ensure that data is encrypted when being sent over the Wireless Local Area Network (WLAN) to protect the confidentiality and integrity of the data;
      b) Ensure that WLAN segment is placed in a dedicated network segment within GEN. Access control mechanisms shall be put in place to only allow authorised network traffic from the WLAN to access the resources within GEN; and
      c) Ensure that intrusion detection system is in place with minimally the following features:
         
        i) Detection of Wireless Access Point (AP) impersonation;
        ii) Detection of rogue AP; and
        iii) Detection of attempted wireless attacks, such as access point spoofing and invalid address combination.
           
    5.4
    Level 0
    		To prevent threat entries from remote access to GEN, Agencies shall:
         
      a) Use Central VPN Service such as SG-VPN and GoMAX for networks that are connected to SGNet; and 
      b) Implement VPN solution with security and access controls for networks that are not connected to SGNet (such as Agency isolated networks).
         
    5.4/S1
    Level 0
    		For VPN implementation on GEN that are not connected to SGNet, Agencies shall:
           
      a) Put in place strong authentication with layered security mechanisms and controls for remote access connections into the networks based on the security risks so they can protect Government Resources from unauthorised access and disclosure. Agencies shall minimally implement the use of PKI certificates to strengthen the authentication and use two-factor authentication (2FA) for remote access except for mobile GFE. Layered security mechanisms and controls include firewall that inspects traffic from gateway to GEN; 
      b) Terminate all remote access connections in a dedicated network segment within the network and restrict access to only systems and services allowed by the Agencies; 
      c) Implement strong encryption for remote access into GEN;
      d) Make sure that the security controls in Personal Computers are checked before granting remote access to GEN:
         
        i) Only authorised Government Furnished Equipment (GFE) shall be used for remote access connection to GEN;
        ii) The GFEs are running up-to-date anti malware, where such protection can be technically implemented;
        iii) The GFEs are updated with up-to-date security patches; and
        iv) The GFEs are installed with personal firewalls, where such protection can be technically implemented;
           
      e) Make sure that remote access connections are not perpetual or to re-authenticate remote users to the VPN gateway on a periodic basis (such as every four hours); 
      f) Set the maximum number of consecutive failed authentication attempts before account lockout for remote access into GEN; and
      g) Make sure that split tunnelling is not implemented.
         
     
    "Strong encryption" refers to industry-accepted open standard algorithms used with appropriate key lengths, and proprietary algorithms must be avoided.

    Level 0
    		To reduce the risks of data exfiltration and ensure secured site-to-site communications, Agencies shall:

        ii) For Agency site that needs to access WOG ICT Infrastructure Services

      b) Implement their own WAN solution with strong encryption for the following use cases:
         
        i) For Agency sites not covered under the Central Government Managed-WAN service to connect back to Agency HQ (e.g. Agency overseas site connectivity to HQ). These Agency Networks shall not have any connectivity to other WAN or MPLS cloud;
        ii) Between Agency isolated networks that do not require connectivity to SGNet; or
        iii) Between Agency sites and Government Data Centres for out-of-band data replication.

    Level 0

    b) Make sure their business partners put in place security measures for their connections to SGNet, to allow only authorised network traffic within SGNet. The security measures shall minimally include the use of dedicated firewalls to connect to the SGNet Wide Area Network routers only;

      e) Before installing any software on endpoint devices (except for iOS mobile devices), to first scan for malware using the latest update of anti-virus software and signatures;

      g) Install host-based firewall software on their endpoint devices (except for GoMAX devices) and make sure that the firewall software is updated with the latest software version, rules and policies;

      i) Configure their centrally managed host-based firewalls to:
         
        i) Disallow users from turning off the personal computer firewall;
        ii) Create, distribute and enforce firewall policies for all users and user groups; and
        iii) Ensure that only authorised applications or ports are allowed.
           
      j) Implement processes to review Agencies’ centrally managed host-based firewall rules in their Personal Computers every 12 months to remove rules that are overly permissive, obsolete or redundant;
      k) Install host intrusion protection and host integrity check, except for mobile devices. Make sure that both host intrusion protection and host integrity check are promptly updated with the latest policies;
      l) Make sure that host intrusion protection and host integrity check are memory-resident and are on at all times when the endpoint device is switched on, except for endpoint devices meant for ICT security vulnerability assessment or ICT security penetration testing;
      m) Make sure that host integrity check is performed before allowing endpoint devices that meet the minimum security standards to connect to Government Enterprise Networks;
      n) Make sure that standard user accounts do not have rights to modify or disable any configuration (including policy settings and rules) on the anti-virus software, host-based firewall, host intrusion protection and host integrity check;
      o) Disable all guest accounts and other unnecessary user-accounts;
      p) Set screen saver ‘time-out’ to prompt for user password to prevent access by unauthorised personnel;
      q) Set boot sequence to internal (primary) storage only (in other words, preventing boot from external or removable storage media and the network);
      r) Disable auto-run (if the auto-run setting is available);
      s) Use clear markings that are not easily removed, to show the contact number of the Agency to recover lost endpoint devices. An example is ‘Please call XXXX XXXX if found’;
      t) Enable full internal (primary storage) hard disk encryption;
      u) Switch off peer-to-peer file shares; and
      v) Disable mapping of native network file shares having remote code invocation functionality (ability to invocate the remote server in executing malicious codes that are uploaded to the server) such as Server Message Block (SMB)-based network file share.

      b) Make sure that contractors have access only to equipment, applications and services needed for their work; and
      c) Ensure timely provisioning and de-provisioning of necessary devices, accounts and access rights used by contractors.
         
    6.3
    Level 0
    		To maintain control of the configuration of all GFE, Agencies shall provide users with standard accounts without administrative rights, and have separate administrator accounts with 2-factor authentication for users authorised to perform work on GFE, such as system configuration, software installation and troubleshooting.
         
    6.4
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall review the need on a periodic basis to implement an enterprise solution to backup and recover Government data stored on endpoint devices so that users can continue working in the event of loss or malfunction of these devices. Adequate protection mechanisms must be in line with the classification levels of the data as outlined in the Singapore Government Instructions for Security of Classified Information. Portable storage media shall not be the primary means for enterprise backup.
       
    6.5
    Level 0
    		To ensure compatibility and conformance to standards, Personal Computers with access to WOG ICT central services, such as SG-Mail, SG-VPN, shall be procured centrally from the Government PC and Printer Bulk Tender (PCBT) and be loaded with the Government Standard Image Build (GSIB).
       
    6.6
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall manage and protect their mobile Government Furnished Equipment (GFE) that are used to store, process, or access Government data from unauthorised access.
       
    6.6/S1
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall manage their mobile GFE using a central Mobile Device Management (MDM) solution, with the exception of those mobile GFE that are only used for research and development purposes.
       
    6.6/S2
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall use passwords and/or biometrics for unlocking their mobile GFE; weak authentication mechanisms such as facial recognition on Android devices, or swipe patterns shall not be used.
       
    6.6/S3
    Level 0
    		For mobile GFE that access Government networks, Agencies shall configure their mobile GFE to:
         
      a) Connect to the Government networks using Virtual Private Networks (VPNs) such as SG-VPN and Agency-managed VPNs;
      b) Use multi-factor authentication for the establishment of their VPN connections;
      c) Disable split tunnelling upon the establishment of VPN sessions; and
      d) Prohibit sharing of VPN sessions.
       
    6.6/S4
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall not allow the use of biometrics or any other weak authentication mechanisms to grant access to Government networks, systems, applications, services, or resources provisioned in the mobile GFE.
       
    6.6/S5
    Level 0
    		For mobile GFE that are managed centrally, Agencies shall ensure that the following requirements are met before granting their GFE access to the Government networks:
         
      a) Patch status of mobile GFE are up-to-date; and
      b) Configurations and properties of mobile GFE which are supported; jailbroken/rooted devices shall not be supported.
       
    6.6/S6
    Level 0
    		For mobile GFE that are managed centrally, Agencies shall configure their MDM and mobile GFE to:
         
      a) Use authorised secure storage compartments to store Government data;
      b) Encrypt Government data prior to performing a backup;
      c) Lock screen after a period of inactivity; this period shall be no longer than five minutes;
      d) Wipe Government data upon ten failed logon attempts;
      e) Enable remote wipe or lock options; and
      f) Remove Government data and access to government networks when mobile GFE is detected to be jailbroken/rooted.
       
    6.6/S7
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall remote wipe or lock the mobile GFE when it is reported to be lost or stolen; the status of the remote wipe or lock shall be tracked to ensure that they have been successfully triggered.
       
    6.6/S8
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall ensure that their users do not back up Government data stored on mobile GFE to non-Government devices or cloud storage services.
       
    6.6/S9
    Level 0
    		For mobile GFE that are managed centrally, Agencies shall review every three months their mobile applications installed on their mobile GFE to identify vulnerable or unauthorised applications; Agencies shall remove these identified applications within five working days upon the completion of review.
     
    6.6/G1
    Level 2
    		Agencies should consider checking if their Android devices’ biometric authentication mechanism meet the following guidelines:
       
      a) Android Compatibility Definition Document (CCD); or
      b) Fingerprint HAL Guidelines.

    7.2/S2##C11
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall maintain segregation of duties between administrators of systems and log management systems (LMS) so that administrators of systems will not be able to modify the data stored in the LMS. For CIIs, Agencies who are CII owners shall ensure that duties relating to system administration and database administration for the CII are segregated and assigned to different persons.
       
    7.2/S3
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall only permit authorised personnel, such as auditors, to view security-related event logs; these personnel shall also not be allowed to modify or delete the logs.
       
    7.2/S4C11
    Level 0
    		For both CII and SII, Agencies shall implement Database Activity Monitoring (DAM) tools to protect their systems’ databases against anomalous or unauthorised activities; For both CII and SII, Agencies shall monitor for bulk queries that exceed a predetermined threshold of data to be retrieved and trigger an alert for investigation when any such bulk query is detected.
       
    7.2/S5
    Level 0
    		For both CII and SII, Agencies shall implement Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR) tools to protect their systems from advanced threats.
       
    7.2/S6##C9
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall store security-related logs generated by their systems, in their raw device log format to support cybersecurity detection and response for:
     
     
    On-Premise systems Cloud systems
    i) a period of 12 calendar months for the security-related logs that are not forwarded to GCSOC; or

    ii) a minimum period of three calendar months for the security-related logs that are forwarded to
    GCSOC. For CII systems, Agencies shall also ensure that they store the required security-related logs
    for a minimum period of 12 months    		 a period of 12 calendar months for security-related logs that are forwarded to GCC central logging servers, regardless if they are being forwarded to GCSOC.
    7.2/S7
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall store security-related event logs in human-readable presentations such as ASCII or UTF-8 encoded text files.
       
    7.2/S8##C9
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall protect log files from unauthorised modification or deletion. Agencies shall stream and store security-related logs that are generated by their systems to their dedicated log management systems.
       
    7.2/S9
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall sanitise classified or sensitive entity information, such as hostnames, IP addresses, or Personally Identifiable Information (PII), in logs before sharing them with any third parties
         
    7.2/S10C11
    Level 0
    		Agencies shall review their system’s security-related event logs to identify potential security violations or breaches by all accounts; the scope and review frequency shall be approved by the appropriate approving authority of residual risks upon the Agencies’ completion of a risk assessment for their systems; For CII, Agencies shall establish mechanisms and processes to monitor the activities of each account, including behavioural patterns, for any anomalies and to trigger an alert for investigation when any anomaly is detected.
       
    7.2/S11C11
    Level 0
    		For CII, Agencies shall review their logging mechanisms, processes and baseline, at least once every 12 months, or whenever there are changes to their logging mechanisms and processes, to verify that their logging mechanisms, processes and baseline still work as intended and have not been tampered with; these mechanisms includes those that generate, transmit store, analyse, and dispose of log data.

      c) Protection measures, such as encryption, access control and backup, of event logs.
